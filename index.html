            <h2>Prediction Markets: Price = Probability</h2>

            <div class="columns-2">

                <div>

                    <h3>What is a Prediction Market?</h3>

                    <p>A prediction market is an exchange-traded market created for the purpose of trading contracts whose value is determined by the **outcome of an uncertain event**. Unlike betting, prediction markets are financial instruments that leverage the **"Wisdom of Crowds"** principle, often proving to be more accurate than polls or expert opinions.</p>

                    <p>You are trading a **Share** in an event's outcome. This share will pay out **\$1.00** if the outcome occurs, and **\$0.00** if it does not.</p>

                </div>

                <div>

                    <h3>The Core Concept: Price Reflects Probability</h3>

                    <div class="concept-box">

                        <h4>\$0.00 to \$1.00 Range</h4>

                        <p>The price of a contract is always between \$0.00 and \$1.00. This price is the market's collective belief in the probability of that event happening.</p>

                        <ul class="probability-list">

                            <li>**\$0.10** per share = **10%** Probability</li>

                            <li>**\$0.50** per share = **50%** Probability (Even Chance)</li>

                            <li>**\$0.85** per share = **85%** Probability (Very Likely)</li>

                        </ul>

                        <p>If you believe the probability is higher than the current price, you **Buy** shares. If you believe it's lower, you **Sell** shares.</p>

                    </div>

                </div>
